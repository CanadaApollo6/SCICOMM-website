Frequently Asked Questions

What is SCICOMM?

SCICOMM is a final project given to undergraduate biology students enrolled in Environmental Ecology at the University of Dayton. It is assigned at the beginning of the semester and due at the end.

What does SCICOMM stand for or mean?

SCICOMM is an abbreviation of two words: Scientific Communication.

What is the goal of SCICOMM?

The goal of SCICOMM is to take complex but important scientific topics and explain them to a more general audience. It also serves as a way for the involved students to learn how to better communicate their own scientific knowledge to those without the same background as them.

Is every SCICOMM project featured on this site?

No. Every student who has work featured here voluntarily did so. While there are more students and more projects, many chose their own ways to get their work into the world. This was just one option for students to use.

What were the requirements for the projects?

The projects were very open-ended for students. They were allowed to take on any topic, medium, and audience.

Is this website a SCICOMM project?

Yes! This website, along with the first 6 "projects" on the home page, act as a single project. The idea to add other projects to the site came later.

How was this website made?

While I originally thought about making this site a ReactJS application, I realized that that would be overkill for a site that only needed to display information only. Instead, the base design of this site uses a free template from HTML5 UP, and is just HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript. I modified and added in all of these areas as needed.
